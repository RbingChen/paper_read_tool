A. Content Understanding

This text provides an introductory overview of behavioral neuroscience, a field that examines the relationship between brain function and behavior. It uses vivid analogies to explain complex concepts, making it accessible for learners. The core structure revolves around seven foundational topics, each metaphorically compared to elements of a city or machine. This approach helps demystify the brain's workings: it depicts the brain as a dynamic "central processing unit" that outputs behavior, with regions like the cerebral cortex acting as a "metropolis" for cognition. The text emphasizes the brain's adaptability through neuroplasticity and highlights how disorders arise from malfunctions. Overall, it fosters appreciation for neuroscience by linking abstract ideas to everyday analogies, reinforcing that understanding these concepts is key to unlocking the brain's secrets.

D. Term Identification

  • Neuroanatomy (神经解剖学): The study of the structure and organization of the nervous system, particularly the brain. It includes regions like the cerebral cortex (for cognition), limbic system (for emotion), and brainstem (for basic functions).
  • Neurophysiology (神经生理学): The branch of neuroscience focusing on how neurons function, including electrical signaling (action potentials) and chemical signaling (neurotransmitters at synapses).
  • Sensory Systems (感觉系统): Neural pathways that gather external information via senses (e.g., vision, hearing), acting as input channels to the brain.
  • Motor Systems (运动系统): Neural mechanisms that convert brain signals into physical actions, enabling movement and response execution.
  • Long-term potentiation (LTP) (长时程增强): A process where repeated neural stimulation strengthens synaptic connections, enhancing learning and memory storage. Formulaically, it can be represented as increased synaptic efficacy: \( \Delta w = \eta (x \cdot y) \), where \( w \) is synaptic weight, \( \eta \) is learning rate, and \( x, y \) are neuron activities.
  • Consolidation (巩固): The process of stabilizing and transferring memories from short-term to long-term storage in the brain.
  • Retrieval (检索): The act of recalling stored memories from long-term storage when needed.
  • Reward System (奖励系统): A neural network, involving areas like the nucleus accumbens, that reinforces behaviors through pleasure and motivation.
  • Nucleus Accumbens (伏隔核): A brain region central to the reward system, involved in processing pleasure and addiction.
  • Amygdala (杏仁核): An almond-shaped structure in the limbic system responsible for emotional processing, fear responses, and memory.
  • Neuroplasticity (神经可塑性): The brain's ability to reorganize neural pathways in response to learning, experience, or injury, enabling adaptation and recovery.
  • Neurological Disorders (神经疾病): Conditions affecting the nervous system, including neurodegenerative diseases (e.g., Alzheimer's, Parkinson's) and psychiatric disorders (e.g., depression, anxiety), often due to structural or chemical imbalances.
