Web agents The development of autonomous web agents has witnessed significant progress from both proprietary and open-source communities (Zhang et al., 2025a). Proprietary systems like Deep-Research (OpenAI, 2025a), Doubao with Deep Think (Doubao, 2025), and Grok-3 (x.ai, 2025) have demonstrated superhuman performance in complex web navigation and information synthesis tasks, but their internal architectures and training methodologies remain opaque, impeding collaborative research. In contrast, open-source projects such as WebDancer (Wu et al., 2025a), WebThinker (Li et al., 2025c), and R1-Searcher (Song et al., 2025), adopting the ReAct framework (Yao et al., 2023), have made strides in simpler tasks yet face a substantial performance gap in benchmarks requiring sophisticated non-linear reasoning. In terms of training methodologies, the foundational principles of training dynamics outlined by (Xu et al., 2019; 2024; 2025) offer crucial insights, informing hyperparameter tuning and algorithmic design by highlighting the decisive impact of the training approach on models’ generalization ability. However, while many studies use Supervised Fine-Tuning (SFT) following the ReAct paradigm, pure SFT agents struggle with generalization in adaptive contexts (Zheng et al., 2025; Zhang et al., 2025b). Reinforcement learning-based methods Song et al. (2025); Zheng et al. 7 Conclusion In this work, we propose WebSailor. From the perspective of uncertainty reduction in information seeking, we analyze why previous open-source web agents have not reached the level of proprietary systems. Our contributions span from QA construction, comprehensive training data synthesis, RFT cold start, to improved efficiency in RL algorithms, leading to a full agentic post-training pipeline. WebSailor demonstrates strong performance on both simple and complex information seeking benchmarks, exhibiting reasoning and tool-use capabilities that surpass human levels. We believe that the key to agentic post-training lies in further defining more complex tasks with higher uncertainty, as well as achieving more effective and efficient RL training. In the future, we will continue to explore how to further enhance agent capabilities based on open-source models, not only in the domain of information seeking, but also in pursuing the goal of general “superhuman” performance across more dimensions.
7 结论 本文提出WebSailor。基于信息搜索中的不确定性降低视角，我们分析了先前开源网络代理未能达到专有系统水平的原因。贡献涵盖问答构建、综合训练数据合成、RFT冷启动（RFT cold start）到强化学习算法效率改进，形成完整代理训练后流程。WebSailor在简单和复杂信息搜索基准上均表现优异，展现出超越人类水平的推理和工具使用能力。 我们认为代理训练后的关键在于定义更高不确定性的复杂任务，并实现更高效强化学习训练。未来将持续探索基于开源模型增强代理能力，不仅在信息搜索领域，更追求跨维度的通用“超人”性能目标。

摘要总结

本文系统分析了现代AI基准测试的演进（从GAIA到BrowseComp的Level 3复杂性）和网络代理发展现状，揭示专有系统（如Deep-Research）与开源代理（如WebDancer）在复杂任务上的性能差距。作者提出WebSailor解决方案，通过创新训练流程（含QA构建、数据合成、RFT冷启动和RL效率优化）实现超越人类水平的性能。核心结论指出：1）开源代理的主要瓶颈在于非线性推理泛化能力；2）强化学习的训练稳定性是关键挑战；3）未来需通过更高不确定性任务定义和高效RL训练实现通用超人性能。

关键术语解释

  • Level 3复杂性（Level 3 complexity）：基准测试的最高难度等级，特征包括实体强耦合、故意信息混淆和高初始不确定性，代表作为BrowseComp-en/zh
  • ReAct框架（ReAct framework）：开源网络代理常用架构，整合推理(Reasoning)与行动(Acting)，但在复杂非线性任务中表现受限
  • 监督微调（Supervised Fine-Tuning, SFT）：基于标注数据的模型调优方法，在自适应场景中泛化能力不足
  • RFT冷启动（RFT cold start）：WebSailor的关键技术，指在缺乏历史交互数据时初始化代理的策略
  • 代理训练后（Agentic post-training）：模型基础训练后的专门优化阶段，聚焦工具使用和决策能力提升
  • 信息混淆（Information obfuscation）：Level 3任务的核心设计策略，通过故意隐藏/扰乱信息增加探索难度
